Jumbo Loans

+ What Is a Jumbo Loan?

• A loan is considered to be a jumbo loan if the amount borrowed is greater than $417,000 for all statesâ€”except Hawaii and Alaska, where it is $625,500

+ What plans exist for a Jumbo Loan?

There are four different types of plans for a Jumbo Loan. We offer :

• 30 year fixed
• 15 year fixed
• 5/1 ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
• 7/1 ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)

+ 30 or 15 year Fixed

Description

 This type of loan has monthly payments that remain the same for the entire loan term after which time the loan is paid in full. The monthly payment is based on an interest rate which does not change over the term of the loan (hence the term "fixed rate").

Benefits

 • Stable Monthly Payments

+ 5/1 and 7/1 ARM's (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)

Description

 This type of loan has monthly payments that are based on a 30 year repayment schedule. The rate stays fixed for the first 5 or 7 years (depending on chosen term), and then adjusts annually thereafter. The rate in which it adjusts to will be based on the index it is tied to and the margin. Because the interest rate can change after the fixed period, the monthly payment may also change.

As an example a 5/1 arm would have a fixed interest rate for the first five years and adjust annually thereafter for the remaining 25 years.

Benefits

 • Lower payments than fixed rate mortgages during initial fixed period.
• Short term plans for ownership.
